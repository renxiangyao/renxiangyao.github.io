COVID-19: Insights of NZ Retail Industry and Trends observed in Supermarket & Grocery Industry

Posted on 2020-10-17 In Industry Insights Views:

[Image Source]

New Zealand Retail industry has had steady and strong growth after 2008 GFC (Global Financial Crisis). For last 12 years, NZ Retail industry CAGR is around 4.1%. The COVID-19 pandemic put a sudden brake on NZ Retail industry due to lockdown and Retails sales plummet during the lockdown period. But the Retail spending observed a V-shaped bouncing back recovery after major lockdown. Supermarket and Grocery industry has been an exception of Retail industry and kept the growing trend since it remained open being the essential business. There are some interesting spending behaviors observed from more granular data.

In this blog, I looked into NZ Retail Trade Quaterly Survey and Electronic Card Transactions Monthly data from Stats NZ to illustrate some insights summarised from data analysis. In the end, I will also briefly talk about some trends I observed in Supermarket industry.

  2. NZ Electronic Card Trasaction (Monthly) - link to download

CAGR

As mentioned earlier, For the last 12 years, NZ Retail industry has experienced CAGR at 4.1% overall. Supmermarket and Grocery industry, which occupies the largest sales proportion% of Retail(21%), also enjoyed a steady and strong growth at CAGR 3.5%. The other 2 major Retail sub industries, Motor Vehicle(14%) and Food & Beverages(12%) both had above 5% CAGR. This is remarkable results considering it all beats NZ GDP annual growth rate average around 3% last decade.

There are 2 other interesting insights. Non-store retailing has the highest CAGR at 10.7%, this could indicate that online shopping is growing really fast in NZ. And Fuel retailing has the lowest CAGR at 1.7%, this matches with the
huge unstoppable wave of electrical cars and clean energy uptake in NZ.

COVID-19: Lockdown impact and post Lockdown recovery

Because of Lockdown caused by COVID-19 pandemic, Almost all NZ Retail sub industries sales plummet during the period. The exceptions are Supermarket and Grocery industry, Electrical and electronic goods industry, and Non-store retailing. Supermarket industry kept its growing trend being the essential business. And due to lockdown, people has to work from home, hence we are observing increases in Electronic devices shopping and online shopping.

The proportion of Electric Card Transactions retail in Total retail trade has rised from 50% to 60% last two decades. Hence the Electric Card Transactions could represent majority of transactions in Retail industry. By looking at the Monthly Electric Card Transaction data, we can observe what does post lockdown recovery look like in Retail sub industries before Quaterly Retail Trade Survey comes out yet.

From the monthly electric card spending, we can see that Supermarket industry keeps growing, and other sub industries sales has bounced back significantly except for Tourism and Travel industry since NZ border is still closed.

COVID-19: Spending/Purchase behavior changes due to lockdown


We can observe that during the lockdown, people’s purchasing behaviors has changed. They spend most of the money buying “Consumables” from Supermarket and people are shopping in bulk each time going out for groceries. The ECT (Electronic Card Transaction) mean transaction value surged during lockdown and dropped to normal level after lockdown. This makes sense since people tend to shop groceries in lump sum during lockdown to avoid queueing long time each time and panic-buying due to limited stock available.

Trends observed in Supermarket Industry

This section is not based on data analysis, but rather based on my personal observation. I noticed that NZ Supermarket Industry is quickly adapting to the changes of our way of shopping and living caused by COVID-19 pandemic. In my opinion, this is one of the silver-lining behind this pandemic cloud. It has accelarated the digital transformation of NZ Retail industry in order to survive. In terms of digital transformation in Supermarket industry, I have noticed 2 major trends/patterns:

1. Online Shopping

Countdown was the pioneer in providing online shopping options in NZ Supermarket industry with NZ wide delivery and pick-up option. New World quickly followed but only available to North island, and recently Pak n Save also launched online shopping options for several stores across North island but only “Click & Pick up” services are available.

Online shopping is a critical part in digital transformation, and we have observed that online shopping has increased significantly (the largest CAGR!) in NZ Retail industry over the last decade. This requires the big 3 supermarket brand company to invest, improve, and integrate heavily in CRM, Order Management, Shop Operation Management, and Transportation management systems behind the scene.

2. Contactless Shopping

Pak n Save and New World has “Shop&Go” service available at selected stores within North Island. New World is currently trialing “Shop&Go” at Durham Street at South Island at the moment. Countdown has recently started testing out “Scan&Go” service in Ponsonby store in Auckland where smart scales know what you are buying by utilising AI image recognition technology.

Pak n Save and New World provides a scanner to let you scan and pack your groceries straight into your bags once you swipe your club/member card at the entrance, then pay at dedicated “Shop&Go” checkout counter.

While Countdown let you shop via an app, scanning, bagging and paying for groceries all from the smartphone, and combined with smart scales mentioned earlier, there is no need to go through the checkout counter anymore technically.

Personally, I think Countdown contactless shopping solution is a better strategy for several reasons:

  • Scanners are extra equipment cost and will impact availability due to limited scanners available, but every customer now has a smartphone to use the app
  • Mobile app is more suitable in digital space, there is much more extendbility and imagination space to carry out and integrate customer purchase behavior analysis, in-store path analysis, personal campaign, and customer loyalty program, etc. at later stage
  • True contactless if using a mobile app to pay wihtout involving checkout counters
